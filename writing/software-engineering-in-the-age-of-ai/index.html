Software engineering is moving toward systems judgment

AI is not removing the need for software engineers. It is shifting the center of gravity toward intent, system design, and the ability to shape reliable boundaries.

December 24, 2025

5 min read

On this page

Back to writing

The first time I saw AI solve a programming problem in seconds, I had the same reaction a lot of developers had: part of me was impressed, and part of me was uneasy.

After spending more time actually building with these tools, my answer changed. What I see now is amplification, not replacement.

Execution is accelerating, but intent still decides the outcome

AI is very good at drafting, exploration, summarisation, scaffolding, and repetitive transformations. But the work that matters most has not gone away.

Someone still needs to decide what should be built, what should not be built, how the system should behave under pressure, where the trust boundaries are, and what good means in the context of a real product.

The center of gravity is moving upward

I think software engineering is becoming less about memorising syntax and more about designing systems with intention. That includes abstractions, interfaces, failure modes, orchestration, and reviewing generated work with taste and skepticism.

Agentic systems make the shift more obvious

Once a model can use tools, call external systems, or operate with broader autonomy, the job becomes much less about code generation and much more about control surfaces: what tools exist, what context they get, what actions are allowed, how state is tracked, and how humans intervene.

In other words, the hard part becomes architecture.

Planning is becoming a stronger differentiator

If AI can help execute faster, poor intent becomes more expensive. The clearer you are about goals, boundaries, and success criteria, the better the outcome tends to be.

  • Scoping
  • Sequencing
  • Communicating intent
  • Structuring context
  • Evaluating trade-offs

My conclusion

I do not think the future belongs to people who can memorise the most syntax. I think it belongs to engineers who can understand how the pieces connect, define clear intent, shape good systems, and use AI as a force multiplier instead of a substitute for thought.
